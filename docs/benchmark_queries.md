# Benchmark Queries (5)

## 1

**Question:** In NIPT, what is the role of paternal DNA information?

**Expected answer:** Paternal-inherited SNPs in cfDNA are analyzed to estimate fetal fraction and confirm fetal DNA detection.

**Source:** `domain_md/01_Prenatal_Genome_White_Paper.md`

## 2

**Question:** What genetic factor determines the subtype (severity category) of alpha-thalassemia?

**Expected answer:** The number of damaged or missing alpha-globin genes (HBA1/HBA2 copies) determines the subtype.

**Source:** `domain_md/02_Alpha_Thalassemia_Fact_Sheet_2022.md`

## 3

**Question:** What is the basic human chromosome makeup described here?

**Expected answer:** Humans have 46 chromosomes: 22 pairs of autosomes plus 2 sex chromosomes; females are XX and males are XY.

**Source:** `domain_md/03_Mendelian_Inheritance_Lecture_2023.md`

## 4

**Question:** What is the most common malignant brain tumour in children?

**Expected answer:** Medulloblastoma.

**Source:** `domain_md/04_Brain_Tumours_Factsheet_2022.md`

## 5

**Question:** Why can brain tumours cause headaches and seizures?

**Expected answer:** Tumour growth can raise pressure inside the head by pushing brain tissue or blocking fluid flow, which can trigger symptoms like headaches and seizures.

**Source:** `domain_md/04_Brain_Tumours_Factsheet_2022.md`
